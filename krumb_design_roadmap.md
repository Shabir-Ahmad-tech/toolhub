# Krumb.dev: Next Steps for Design

As your mentor, I want to keep this simple, realistic, and highly organized. You have the logo. Now, we need to build the system around it so you stay consistent and disciplined. 

Here is the detailed roadmap for what we must design next.

## Phase 1: The Brand System (The Foundation)
Before you design a website, you need rules. This keeps your brand looking professional everywhere.

*   **Brand Style Guide (The Rulebook):** This is a document that explains exactly how to use your logo. It shows the minimum size, the spacing around it, and what people are *not* allowed to do with it.
*   **Color Palette:** We have your main green and black. Now we need the exact HEX and RGB codes for your secondary colors (like text colors, borders, and error states).
*   **Typography System:** We chose Space Grotesk and JetBrains Mono. We need to decide the exact sizes and weights for your main titles (H1), sub-titles (H2), and normal paragraph text.

## Phase 2: UI/UX and Website Design (The Core)
Since Krumb.dev is a set of developer tools, the website interface is the most important part.

*   **Component Library:** Design the small pieces first. We need to design the exact look of your buttons, input fields, dropdowns, and error messages. 
*   **Website Layouts:** Design the actual pages. You need a design for the homepage (with the big search/input bar) and a layout for the individual tool pages.
*   **Interactive States:** Design what happens when a user hovers over a button, clicks a link, or gets a success message.

## Phase 3: Marketing and Digital Assets (Getting Noticed)
When you share your tools, they need to look good on the internet.

*   **Social Media Profile Kit:** We need your new square logo sized perfectly for Twitter/X, LinkedIn, and GitHub.
*   **Open Graph (OG) Images:** When someone shares a Krumb.dev link on a chat or social media, an image pops up. We need a strong, branded image for this.
*   **Post Templates:** If you write updates or tips, we need a simple, reusable background template for your social media posts.

## Phase 4: Business Assets (For You and Your Team)
These are the professional touches for your agency and daily work.

*   **Email Signature:** A clean, text-based signature with your new logo for your professional emails.
*   **Presentation Deck:** A slide template (Google Slides or PowerPoint) with your dark mode, high-contrast theme for when you pitch to clients or explain your tools.
